What is Baptism?
Baptism is a symbolic and sacred ritual in Christianity, representing spiritual purification and rebirth. Typically involving the application of water, it signifies a believer's public declaration of faith, cleansing from sin, and identification with the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. Baptism varies among Christian denominations, with some practicing infant baptism as an initiation into the faith, while others emphasize believer's baptism, associating it with a conscious decision to follow Christ. Ultimately, baptism holds profound significance as a visible expression of one's commitment to the Christian faith and community.
