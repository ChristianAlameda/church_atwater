TITHING
What is Tithing?
Tithing is a practice rooted in religious and spiritual traditions, particularly within Christianity. The word "tithe" refers to giving a tenth or 10% of one's income to support the church and its mission. The rationale behind tithing includes:
    Biblical Teaching: Tithing finds its foundation in various biblical passages, such as Malachi 3:10, where believers are encouraged to bring their tithes to the storehouse.
    Supporting the Church: Tithing is a way to financially support the church and its activities, including worship services, community outreach, and ministerial efforts.
    Trust and Obedience: It is considered an act of trust and obedience to God's commandments, reflecting a commitment to prioritize spiritual values over material wealth.
    Blessings and Abundance: Some believers view tithing as a way to open themselves up to God's blessings and abundance, both spiritually and materially.g
    Community and Fellowship: Tithing fosters a sense of community and fellowship among believers, contributing to the overall well-being of the church family.
    Stewardship: Tithing promotes the idea of responsible stewardship, acknowledging that all resources ultimately belong to God, and believers are entrusted with their use.
